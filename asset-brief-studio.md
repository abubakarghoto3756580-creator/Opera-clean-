# Opera Clean — Custom Image Asset Brief (STUDIO PHOTOGRAPHY VERSION)
For generation via Midjourney / Nano Banana. All prompts below are rewritten as **pure studio photography** — seamless backdrop, controlled studio lighting rig, no outdoor/lifestyle/environment settings. This keeps every image visually consistent with each other (same backdrop family, same lighting logic) which is actually better for brand cohesion than mixed lifestyle shots.

Brand palette to reference in every prompt: Matte Black #111111, Charcoal #1D1D1D, Gold #C6A86A, Gold Hover #D7BE82, White. Core style phrase to repeat in every prompt: **"studio photography, seamless charcoal-grey backdrop, controlled three-point studio lighting with a warm gold key light, no environment or background context, premium commercial product photography, photorealistic."**

## 1. Hero Section (currently no image)
**Purpose:** Establish luxury trust in the first second.
**Composition:** Straight-on or slight 3/4 studio product shot of a pristine black tailored garment (blazer or abaya fabric) mounted on an invisible/minimal stand, floating against the backdrop.
**Studio setup:** Seamless charcoal-grey infinity cove backdrop, garment lit with a soft key light + gold-gelled rim light from behind to separate it from the backdrop.
**Subject:** Rich black fabric, sharp folds, one small gold detail (button/clasp) catching the rim light.
**Lighting:** Classic studio 3-point setup — soft key light front-left, fill light front-right (lower intensity), warm gold rim/hair light from behind.
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9, min 2400×1350px.

**Prompt:** "Studio product photograph of a pristine black tailored garment with sharp fabric folds, floating against a seamless charcoal-grey studio backdrop, three-point studio lighting with soft key light, subtle fill, and a warm gold rim light separating the garment from the background, single small gold button catching the light, premium commercial studio photography, photorealistic, 16:9"

## Why studio-only works better here
Every image now shares the same backdrop family, the same lighting logic, and the same "product photography" language — so even generated separately, they will feel like one consistent shoot rather than a mismatched stock-photo grab-bag. This also makes it much easier to swap in real photos later (shot in an actual studio tent with the same backdrop) without a visual mismatch.
